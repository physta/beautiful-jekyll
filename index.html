The Kinetic Collective Model (KCM), developed by the nanoTransport group of the Universitat Autonoma de Barcelona, is a generalization of the Guyer and Krumhansel solution of the phonon Boltzman Transport Equation. KCM allows to compute the thermal conductivity of semiconductors in a fast and low memory way from first principles calculations.

The KCM:

  • Properly accounts for the effect of normal scattering processes.
  • Uses first principles relaxation times.
  • Allows fast calculations of thermal conductivity with low memory and time usage.
  • Defines an hydrodynamic heat fulx equation able to use in finite elements simulations for thermal calculations in complex geoemtries (See the hydrodynamic equation in Theory).
